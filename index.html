49th International Invention Show (INOVA-Croatia 2025)

Topic: Extraction of Cinnamaldehyde from Vietnamese Cinnamon for the Formulation of Emulsion Gel to Inhibit Biofilm Formation on Medical Devices

My Role:

  • Built and developed research ideas: Focus on Vietnamese cinnamon bark (Cinnamomum loureiroi) - a valuable resource but underutilised in practical uses.
  • Conducted key experiments:
    • Used steam distillation to extract cinnamaldehyde.
    • Developed the cinnamaldehyde-containing nanoemulsion using the Phase Inversion Point (PIP) method.
    • Employed formulation materials including Tween 80 (surfactant), propylene glycol (cosurfactant), and distilled water.
    • Incorporated the nanoemulsion into a gel base using Carbopol 940/HPMC, glycerin, and triethanolamine (neutralizer), achieving the complete emulsion gel formulation suitable for medical device application.

RESEARCH & PROJECTS

International Journal of Multidisciplinary on Science and Management

Status: Published (Vol 2, Issue 3) on ijmsm.org/ijmsm-v2i3p114.html | Focus: To Lich River & Wastewater Treatment

My works are associated with the daily, ongoing problems I have seen, especially after gaining a better understanding of chemicals and materials. Therefore, I co-author in the research article “Water Pollution Status and Solutions in Vietnam”, published in Volume 2, Issue 3 of the International Journal of Multidisciplinary on Science and Management (IJMSM 2025), relating to the To Lich River, which used to be the most contaminated and stinky river in Hanoi.

Research Content:

  • Analyzed major sources of water contamination in Vietnam, including industrial waste, petroleum spills, domestic wastewater, soil erosion, agricultural runoff, and plastic pollution.
  • Proposed modern water purification solutions, such as integrated treatment technologies, hybrid machine-learning models, and chemical purification methods based on chlorine disinfection and spectrophotometric analysis.
  • Conducted case studies in Hanoi with a focus on evaluating the Yen Xa Wastewater Treatment Plant and the master plan for revitalizing the To Lich River.

My Role:

  • Summarized Section II: Related Works on water pollution in Vietnam.
  • Contributed to data analysis and present purification methods, including physical, chemical, and advanced technological approaches.
  • Developed content for the subsection on the Application of Integrated Technologies in Section IV: Water Purification Methods and Section V: Case Study of Water Purification in Hanoi, Vietnam.

Note: The Yen Xa Wastewater Treatment Plant successfully improved water quality on schedule by the end of August 2025. At that time, the research article had been published. It could have been more comprehensive if it had assessed the treatment plant's efficiency and its long-term results. In the future, if possible, I would love to update the water quality status and evaluate the methods the Yen Xa Wastewater Treatment Plant has implemented to achieve such a great result.


Biodegradable Plastic Project

Another time is when I read an article about landfill sites in Vietnam being overloaded. Unlike the waste-sorting systems in Singapore and the EU, Vietnam's waste treatment system remains underdeveloped and relies mainly on burying waste.

Therefore, I found another way to reduce the amount of plastic discharged into the environment by promoting biodegradable plastic bags. By making a bioplastic bag from starch derived from Canada edulis Ker-Gawl, I understand better the process of creating a bioplastic bag and its degradation mechanism in soil.
